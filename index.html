About

I’m an aerospace engineering student at the University of Michigan focused on controls, sensing, and hands-on prototyping. I like taking a system from first-principles modeling to hardware that performs the way the math says it should.

Recently, I helped build a 1U CubeSat ADCS imaging testbed, integrating IMU, magnetometer, and optical sensors with a reaction wheel and torque rods. I implemented TRIAD/EKF attitude estimation and mode logic for detumble, pointing, and desaturation, then verified performance with hardware-in-the-loop tests.

Beyond space systems, I prototype fast and lightweight vehicles. I co-designed a Titan “air-puck” concept (CATIA + STAR-CCM+), tuned it with bench data, and proved it on a course. I also assembled and competed with a skirted hovercraft, balancing mass, flow, and power to hit time and payload targets. For energy access work, I helped design a low-cost wind generator using locally available materials and measured output, efficiency, and TSR under load.

I’m happiest where controls meets hardware—sensors on the bench, scripts on the laptop, and a clear test plan to close the loop from model → build → verify.
